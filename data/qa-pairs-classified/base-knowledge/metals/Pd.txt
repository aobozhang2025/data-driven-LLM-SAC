question
chemical properties of Palladium (Pd)

answer
At the heart of palladium's catalytic prowess is its electron configuration, [Kr] 4d¹⁰, which contributes to its notable chemical characteristics. In the context of single-atom catalysis, the following properties are particularly significant:

Variable Oxidation States: A defining feature of palladium in single-atom catalysts is its ability to readily cycle through a wide range of oxidation states, from Pd(IV) to even negative states like Pd(-II) in some systems. This facile change in oxidation state is fundamental to its catalytic activity, allowing it to actively participate in the transfer of electrons during chemical reactions. The single-atom nature of the catalyst often leads to unique and dynamic charge transfer between the palladium atom and the support material.

Lewis Acidity: As an isolated, positively charged species on a support, a single palladium atom acts as a strong Lewis acid, an electron-pair acceptor. This property is crucial for activating reactant molecules by withdrawing electron density, thereby making them more susceptible to chemical transformation.

Hydrogen Absorption and Activation: Palladium is renowned for its exceptional ability to absorb large volumes of hydrogen gas. As single atoms, this property is harnessed to efficiently activate hydrogen molecules, breaking the H-H bond and facilitating hydrogenation reactions.

chemical properties of Palladium(II) Nitrate Dihydrate (Pd(NO₃)₂·2H₂O)

answer
This salt is a widely used Pd precursor due to its good solubility in water and various organic solvents, facilitating its uniform impregnation onto the support material. For instance, it is a common choice for creating palladium single-atom catalysts on nitrogen-doped carbon supports.
