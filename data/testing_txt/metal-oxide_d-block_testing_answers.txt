The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation with metal precursor PtCl2 and support Y zeolite

The synthesis procedures are as followed:
1. PtCl2  and excess ethylenediamine (EDA) were mixed in distilled water and stirred until completely dissolved, in which EDA acted as both ligand to adjust the size of the precursors and protective agent to prevent metal precipitation in the intense alkaline hydrothermal conditions for Y zeolite crystallization. 
2. Then, NaOH, NaAlO2, SiO2, and directing agent were introduced into the aqueous solution, reaching the final molar composition of 3.36 Na2O:1.0 Al2O3:8.4 SiO2:250  H2O:0.015 PtCl2: 2.0EDA, and the final pH value of the  starting gel was over 14. 
3. After crystallization at 100 °C for 12 h without stirring, the obtained products were washed with distilled water adequately, followed by calcination in static air and flowing H2 successively.

The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation with metal precursor Rh(III)-acetylacetonate and support MgO

The support MgO is prepared as followed:
1. 100 mL of a Mg(II) methoxide solution (7−8% in methanol, SigmaAldrich) was diluted in 1 L of toluene (technical grade). 
2. Next, Milli-Q treated water was added dropwise at RT, and the suspension was stirred overnight to enable a complete hydrolysis of the alkoxide precursor. 
3. The resulting gel was collected by centrifugation, dried at 353 K, and calcined under air flow in a fixed-bed reactor at 773 K for 5 h (heating rate, 2 K/min). 

After the support MgO is prepared, the catalyst is prepared as followed:
1. Rh(III)-acetylacetonate (Sigma-Aldrich, 97%) was dissolved in acetone and impregnated on support CeO2 in a rotary evaporator (430 mbar, 313 K). 
2. The solid was further dried at 353 K in an oven overnight and ground into a powder. 
3. The resulting powder was calcined in an uncapped porcelain dish (50 mm diameter) at 1073 K under a stagnant air atmosphere for 10 h using a temperature ramp from RT of 2 K/min. 
4. Finally, the catalyst was ground and sieved to retain particles in the 100−200 μm size range. 
5. The metal content was adjusted to achieve a preset surface-specific Rh content (after the high-temperature annealing treatment) of 1.0 ± 0.2 Mat/nm2.

The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation 

The MFI seeds are prepared as followed:
1. 16 g TPAOH  solution and 14.4 g TEOS solution were added into a 50 mL plastic bottle and stirred for 12 h. 
2. Then the mixture solution was transferred into 100 mL Teflon-lined stainless-steel autoclave and static crystallization for 12 h at 120 oC. 
3. After cooling down to the room temperature, as-synthesized  ultrasmall MFI seed was collected by centrifugation, washed with distilled water for four times and then freeze-dried for overnight. 
4. The final seed was calcinated in the air at 550 oC for 6 h to remove the organic template agent (TPAOH). 

After the MFI seeds are prepared, the catalyst is synthesized as followed:
1. First, 0.382 g NaOH and 0.216 g NaAlO2 was dissolved in 22.84 g distilled water. 
2. Then 0.25 mL 0.09 M [Pd(NH2CH2CH2NH2)2]Cl2 aqueous solution was added by dropwise into mixture solution. 
3. Subsequently, 5.765 g colloidal silica was slowly added into above solution and stirred for 5 h at room temperature. 
4. After that, 0.185 g ultrasmall MFI seeds were added into aluminosilicate gel and stirred for another 1 h. 
5. The mixture solution was transferred into 100 mL Teflon-lined stainless-steel autoclave and static crystallization for 24 h at 180 oC. 
6. After cooling down to the room temperature, as-synthesized Pd@ZSM-5 was collected by centrifugation,  washed with distilled water for four times and dried at 80 oC for overnight. 
7. The final sample was calcinated in the air at 400 oC for 2 h to remove ethanediamine.

7. Finally, the dried powder was calcined at 400 °C in a muffle furnace for 4 h.

The catalyst is prepred by impregnation with metal precursor H2IrCl6 and support precursor anatase ZnO

The catalyst is prepared as followed:
1. 50 mg of commercial ZnO was dispersed in 10 ml of 20% methanol aqueous solution in a 20 ml gas-tight vial under sonication for 10 min. 
2. 2.2 mg of H2IrCl6 are diluted with H2O to make 1 ml of aqueous Ir precursor solutions. 
3. The ZnO dispersion and Ir precursor solution were purged with Ar gas for 10 min. 
4. The ZnO dispersion was vigorously stirred and simultaneously irradiated with UV–vis light at an intensity of 100 mW cm–2 using a xenon lamp light source (Newport). 
5. The light was irradiated for 20 min for each sample.  
6. After the ZnO dispersion turned bluish, 0.5 ml of the Ir precursor solution was injected into the ZnO dispersion and vigorously stirred. 
7. After 10 min of stirring, the reaction solution was centrifuged and washed with water and ethanol. 
8. The product was dried in an electric oven at 50 °C overnight

The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation with metal precursor Cu(NO3)2·3H2O

The synthesis procedures are as followed:
1. 875 mg of trimesic acid was dissolved in 40 ml MeOH  (solution A). 
2. Subsequently, 2.43 g of Cu(NO3)2·3H2O and 1 mmol of tantalum ethoxide were dissolved in 40 ml MeOH (solution B). 
3. The Solution A and B were mixing under room temperature overnight. 
4. The as-obtained product was centrifuged and dried in a vacuum oven. 
5. Then, cation exchange process was done with the powder. 
6. In 92 ml of DI water, 120 mg of RuCl3·3H2O were dissolved. 
7. Subsequently, 200 mg of the as-synthesized HKUST-1 powder was homogeneously dispersed in the above solution, followed by vigorous shaking and sonication. 
8. The homogeneous mixture was then stirred in the dark. 
9. After 18 h of stirring, the products were washed five times with DI water and dried in a vacuum oven overnight. 
10. The dried powder was heated at 300 °C for 4 h in a muffle furnace (ramping rate of 5 °C/min). 
11. Thereafter, the calcined product was treated  with 0.5 M H2SO4 at 80 °C for 7 hours to remove the unstable metal species. 
12. Finally, the acidtreated powder was collected and washed three times with DI water, followed by drying in a  vacuum oven.

The catalyst is synthesized by irradiation with metal precursor chloroplatinic acid.

The synthesis procedures are as followed:
1. The chloroplatinic acid was firstly solved in ethanol with 0.05 M. 
2. The solution was dropped onto TiO2 nanoparticles (purchased from Aladdin, China) with a loading amount of ~0.1 ml/mg, followed by ultrasonication for 1 h and dried in vacuum oven at room temperature.  
3. To synthesize Pt species, the precursors-loaded substrates were dispersed in hexane at 0.5 mg/ml under stirring. 
4. The solution was irradiated with a fiber laser for 8 h under stirring, ensuring all the substrates to interact with laser beam. 
5. Finally, the mixture was centrifuged and rinsed several times in ethanol to remove the unreacted salt precursors, and the sample was dried in a vacuum oven.
