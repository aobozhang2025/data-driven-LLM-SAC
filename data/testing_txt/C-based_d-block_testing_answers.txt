The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation with metal precursor CoCl2·6H2O

The synthesis procedures are as followed:
1. 4.3875 g NaCl, 416 mg glucose and 0.03 mmol CoCl2·6H2O were added into 25 mL DI and stirred for 1 h to ensure the homogeneous state of the solution. 
2. The obtained mixture was freeze-dried for 48 h and then ground by agate mortar to form fine powder.  
3. The powder was put in the middle of the tube furnace and flushed with argon gas (Ar) for at least 30 min to eliminate air thoroughly. 
4. The tube furnace was heated at 500 °C with the ramp rate of  5 °C/min and kept for 4 hours under the ammonia gas (NH3) atmosphere (the flow rate of NH3 was  50 sccm). 
5. Dissolve the calcination powder in 2000 mL water and stir overnight to remove NaCl template completely, then dried at 80 °C overnight. 
6. Calcining the black powder under 850 °C by  controlling the heating rate 5 °C/min for 4 hours under the protection of Ar

The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation with precursor H2PtCl6

The support N-doped carbon nanotubes denoted by N-CNT is prepared as followed:
1. First, MnO2 nanowires as the template and oxidant were prepared by the hydrothermal method. 
2. Then 200 mg of MnO2 nanowires were added into 100 mL deionized water  (DIW) (solution A). 
3. Pyrrole (0.51 mL) and aniline (0.39 mL) were added into 100 mL  of 0.05 M H2SO4 solution (solution B). 
4. The above two solutions were mixed under vigorous stirring. 
5. After continuous stirring for 4 h, a black product was obtained, which  was further subjected to rinse with a large amount of DIW to remove the residuary Mn.  
6. Lastly, the as-obtained product was heated to 920 °C for 2 h under N2 flow to yield the  N-CNTs.  

the catalyst is synthesized as followed:
1. The as-synthesized N-CNTs (40.0 mg) were first dispersed in 20 mL DIW under  ultrasonic vibration and magnetic stirring. 
2. A H2PtCl6 solution (2.0 mg/mL in DIW) of  268 μL was next added dropwise into the N-CNTs dispersion under stirring at ambient temperature. 3. After continuous stirring overnight, the solid composite was separated from the suspension by vacuum filtration. 
4. The recovered solid was then dried at 70 °C  for 12 h and reduced in 5% H2/Ar at 160 °C for 0.5 h with a heating rate of 5 °C min-1.

The catalyst is synthesized with metal precursor FeCl3 

The catalyst synthesis procedures are as followed:
1. 0.7 g of 1,2-dicyanobenzene and 16 mg of FeCl3 was mixed with 7 g of a 60 mol% LiCl/ZnCl2  mixture in a Ar-filled glovebox. 
2. The resulting powder was placed in an alumina crucible and heated  with a heating rate of 10 K/min to 800 °C in a tube furnace with a constant Ar flow.

The catalyst is prepared by impregnation with metal precursor CoCl2(PPh3)2 and support ZIF-8

The support ZIF-8 is prepared as followed:
1. Zn(NO3)2∙6H2O (1.069 g, 3.6 mmol) was dissolved in  20 mL methanol. 
2. Then the solution was added into 20 mL of methanol containing 2-methyimidazole (1.161 g, 14.2 mmol). 
3. After vigorously stirring for 5 minutes, the mixed solutions were transferred into a 50 mL Telfon-lined stainless-steel autoclave and subjected to treatment at 120 oC  for 4 h. The autoclave was cooled down to room temperature. 
4. The solid products were collected by centrifugation and washed five times with methanol.
5. Finally, the products were dried at 70 oC under vacuum overnight.  

After the support ZIF-8 is prepared, the catalyst is synthesized as followed:
1. ZIF-8 (100 mg) were dispersed in 30 mL methanol.  
2. Subsequently, the solution was added to 20 mL of methanol containing CoCl2(PPh3)2 (10 mg). 
3. The mixed solutions were vigorously stirred at 40 oC for 16 hours. Afterward, the mixture was cooled down to room temperature. 
4. The solid products (Co-P/ZIF-8) were collected by centrifugation and  washed five times with methanol. 
5. Finally, the Co-P/ZIF-8 was placed in a tube furnace and heated under argon gas flow (100 mL/min) at a heating rate of 5 oC/min to reach a temperature of 950 oC for 3 h after drying the products at 70 oC under vacuum overnight.

The catalyst is prepared as followed:
1. C2H4N4 (12 g), C3H7NO2 (3 g) and Ru(acac)3 (40 mg) were added together to a ball mill jar.

3. 4. The prepared catalyst was named Ru1/NC.

The synthesis procedures are as followed:
1. First, to prepare the nitrogen-doped graphene support (GO–NH2), GO (purchased from CYG and used as received) was mixed with urea (VWR) with a mass ratio of 1 to 10, and further heated in a tube furnace to 500 °C under a gas flow of 100 sccm Ar (UHP, Airgas) within 1 h, and then kept at the same temperature for another 2 h before cooling to room temperature.

6. Finally, the powder was collected.

The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation with metal precursor Rhodium chloride hydrate (RhCl3·xH2O, Rh 38-40 wt.%).

The support porphyrin-based MOF, denoted by pMOF is prepared by the following procedures:
1. 40 mg of zirconium chloride (ZrCl4) was first dissolved in the mixture solution containing 8 mL of N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF) and 0.8 mL of water (H2O). 
2. Then, 800 mg of benzoic acid was added into the above solution, followed by stirring vigorously for 20 min,  producing a yellowish solution.
3. Afterwards, 40 mg of 4,4′,4′′,4′′′-(Porphine-5,10,15,20tetrayl)tetrakis(benzoic acid) (TCPP) was introduced. 
4. The mixture was stirred for another 20 min and transferred to a 50-mL autoclave, which was maintained at 120 °C for 24 h. 
5. The solid product was collected by centrifuge and washed thoroughly by ethanol at least 3 times and dried in a vacuum oven overnight. 
6. A powder in auburn color was collected for further utilizations.  

The catalyst is synthesized by impregnation with metal precursor Rhodium chloride hydrate (RhCl3·xH2O, Rh 38-40 wt.%):
1. 80 mg of pMOF powder was weighed and dispersed in 16 mL of DMF with stirring. 
2. 42 mg of Rhodium chloride hydrate (RhCl3·xH2O, Rh 38-40 wt.%) was dissolved in 0.8 mL of H2O with ultrasonication. 
3. The above solution was mixed and transferred to a 50-mL autoclave, followed by heating to 80 °C for 4 h. 
4. Then, the product was collected by centrifuge and washed thoroughly by ethanol at least 3 times. 
5. To remove impurities and residual solvents in the MOF channels, the  resulting Rh1/pMOF was soaked and rinsed with ethanol and acetone sequentially. 
6. Finally, the solid product was collected by centrifuge and dried in a vacuum oven at 230 °C for 36 h. 
7. To increase the Rh loading, the above synthetic process should be repeated at least 5 times.

The catatyst is synthesized by impregnation with metal precursor Pd(ii) acetylacetonate and support activated carbon

The synthesis procedures are as followed:
1. Activated carbon was initially ground to obtain a powder (150–200 mesh). 
2. Pd(ii) acetylacetonate (Aldrich) was dissolved in the required solvent (2.7 ml). 
3. The solution was then added dropwise, with stirring, to the activated carbon (0.99 g) to obtain a catalyst with a final metal loading of 1 wt.%. 
4. The resulting powder was dried at a temperature 5–10 °C higher than the boiling point of the solvent used for 16 h under a flow of N2. 
5. Wherever possible, extra dry solvents sealed under nitrogen were used.
